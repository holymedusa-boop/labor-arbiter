## Technology Deep Dive: How Digital Humans Became Believable

### The Avatar IV Revolution

The release of **Avatar IV models** in 2025 marked a qualitative leap in digital human technology. Key innovations include:

1. **Diffusion-Based Expression Engine**: Real-time analysis of speech rhythm, tone, and emotion to generate corresponding micro-expressions (smiling, frowning, eyebrow raises)
2. **Multi-Angle Input Support**: Accepts side profiles, half-body, and full-body images—not just frontal portraits
3. **Breathing Simulation**: Subtle chest movement and throat dynamics synchronized with speech patterns
4. **Gesture Generation**: AI-generated hand movements based on text prompts and emotional context

### Training Data & Methodology

## Challenges & Controversies

### The Authenticity Debate

As digital humans become indistinguishable from real people, fundamental questions arise:

- **Trust**: When followers can't tell if they're engaging with a real person or AI, does it constitute deception?
- **Employment**: Digital humans threaten the livelihood of real influencers, actors, and customer service workers
- **Identity**: What does it mean when anyone can clone your appearance and voice?

### Technical Limitations

Despite impressive advances, current digital humans still struggle with:
- **Complex emotions**: Subtle sarcasm, genuine warmth, and spontaneous humor remain challenging
- **Physical interaction**: Manipulating real objects in real-time is not yet possible
- **Extreme angles**: Side profiles and rapid head movements can break the illusion

### Market Structure Risks

Guiji's IPO prospectus reveals concerning concentration:
- **Single customer contributed >50% of 2024 revenue**
- Gross margins declining from 45.8% (2023) to 31.6% (H1 2025)
- Intense price competition from free/open-source alternatives
